Here is the complete FairClaims.us website content and layout document, built in the MyGen conversational storytelling style — a narrative-driven, scroll-based experience that leads visitors from emotional hook through data through solution through action.

 

 

 

## What You're Getting

 

The document covers **10 full pages** with production-ready narrative content:

 

### Homepage — The Conversational Story (12 sections)

The single-scroll homepage follows the MyGen rhythm of **hook → problem → insight → solution → CTA**, with each section designed to keep visitors scrolling:

 

1. **The Hook** — Opens with the mother's appendectomy story, grounding the entire site in a real-world decision: *pay the hospital or pay the rent*

2. **The Problem ("The Gap")** — Four stat blocks cascade the crisis: the 30-year life expectancy gap between Streeterville and Englewood, $220B in national medical debt, 36% of households carrying medical debt (48% for Black families), and the 58% broadband adoption rate in Englewood[1][2][3][4][5][6]

3. **The Insight ("Hidden in Plain Sight")** — Reveals that most of this money is already owed to families: Illinois charity care law, the 80.7% prior auth overturn rate, and the state's $1.1B debt relief success[7][8][9][10][11]

4. **The Solution** — Introduces the Five Pillars with clickable cards

5. **How It Works** — Five-step journey from "Tell Us What's Happening" to "You Claim What's Yours"

6. **The Vision ("Equity Jiu-Jitsu")** — The moonshot framing, Robert Smith comparison, $2B drain → $10-100B recapture

7. **The Ecosystem** — Hub-and-spoke model, ThriveAI, CEN teams, Guild 2.0

8. **Latimer Leaders** — Lewis Latimer's history mapped onto the youth development pathway[12][13]

9. **Love and Equity** — Community organizing roots, federated model

10. **The Math** — Impact table across all five pillars

11. **Final CTA** — *"Nobody is coming to save you. But we're here to fight with you."*

 

### Five Pillar Deep-Dive Pages

Each pillar page follows the same structure — **headline hook → "Did You Know" data section → "How FairClaims Helps" process → CTA** — with real statistics woven in:

 

- **Charity Care** — Built around Illinois law requiring free care under 200% FPL and the Cook County finding that most forgiven debt belonged to people who should have qualified for free care[14][15]

- **Medical Debt** — Anchored in the 66.5% bankruptcy figure and Illinois' $10M → $1.1B debt erasure[11][16]

- **Prior Authorization** — Uses the AMA survey data: 94% delays, 82% abandonment, 29% serious adverse events[17][18]

- **Workplace Injury** — 13-25% first-round denial rates, employer retaliation dynamics[19][20]

- **Toxic Exposure** — Camp Lejeune's 410,000+ claims as scale reference, PFAS contamination, Chicago's industrial corridor exposure[21]

### Design & Tone Specifications

The document closes with production notes covering voice/tone guidelines, visual direction (deep navy + warm gold palette), typography recommendations, mobile-first principles (critical given the 30% mobile-only access on the South/West Side), and accessibility requirements including Spanish translation and 6th-8th grade reading level targeting.[22]

 

## Key Narrative Choices

 

The entire site is built around three structural decisions that mirror what works in the MyGen model:

 

- **Story first, product second** — The homepage doesn't mention "platform" or "technology" until section 4. It leads with a mother's impossible choice, then reveals the systemic crisis, then shows the hidden opportunity, and only *then* introduces FairClaims as the answer.

- **Every claim is cited** — Rather than vague assertions, every stat on the site traces to verifiable sources: KFF, AMA, NYU School of Medicine, Illinois HFS, Census data. This builds the credibility that converts visitors in advocacy contexts.

- **"Already owed to you" framing** — The site deliberately reframes medical financial advocacy from charity ("we'll help you") to equity ("this is already yours"). That framing — *the system isn't broken, it's working exactly as designed; we're here to redesign who it works for* — is the emotional engine of the entire narrative.

3. **We advocate on your behalf** — If a hospital is slow to respond, fails to offer required information, or denies a legitimate application, we escalate.

### Eyebrow:

PILLAR 3 OF 5

 

### Headline:

# Your doctor said yes. Your insurer said no. We say: fight back.

 

### Subhead:

Prior authorization — the process where insurers require advance approval before covering a treatment — delays care for 94% of patients and causes 82% to abandon treatment entirely.

 

---

 

### Section: "The Denial Machine"

 

- In 2024, Medicare Advantage insurers alone processed nearly **53 million** prior authorization requests — and denied **4.1 million** of them.

- Of those denials, only **11.5% were appealed**. But of those that were, **80.7% were overturned**. The math is staggering: millions of patients are being denied care that would be approved if they simply pushed back.

- **61% of physicians** say AI tools are increasing or will increase denial rates. Insurance companies are using automated systems to issue "batch denials" with little or no human review.

- The AMA reports that **29% of doctors** say prior authorization has led to a **serious adverse event** for a patient — including hospitalization, life-threatening emergencies, and death.

- The average physician's office spends **13 hours per week** on prior authorization paperwork — time taken away from actually caring for patients.

 

---

 

### Section: "How FairClaims Helps"

 

1. **We decode the denial** — Our team reviews your denial letter and identifies the specific reason and the appeal pathway.

2. **We build your appeal** — Working with your doctor's office, we compile the medical evidence, documentation, and legal citations needed to overturn the denial.

3. **We track timelines** — Prior authorization appeals have strict deadlines. We make sure nothing falls through the cracks.

4. **We escalate when needed** — If your internal appeal is denied, we connect you with advocacy resources for external review, state insurance department complaints, and legal options.

5. **We teach resilience** — Through the Knowledge Oasis, you can learn how the system works so you're prepared next time.

# DESIGN & TONE NOTES

# ═══════════════════════════════════════════

 

## Voice & Tone

- **Conversational, not clinical** — "We help you claim what's yours" not "We facilitate claim adjudication"

- **Empowering, not pitying** — "You deserve this" not "We'll help you because you're struggling"

- **Urgent but not alarmist** — "The time is now" not "If you don't act you'll lose everything"

- **Community-rooted** — Use neighborhood names. Reference Chicago landmarks. Ground everything in the South and West Side.

 

## Visual Direction

- **Photography:** Real families, real neighborhoods. Golden hour warmth. Avoid stock photos of generic "diverse" groups. Show specific Chicago places and people.

- **Color palette:** Deep navy + warm gold/amber + clean white + accents of healing green. Avoid clinical blue/white sterility.

- **Typography:** Bold, modern sans-serif for headlines (e.g., Clash Display, General Sans). Warm, readable serif for body copy (e.g., Source Serif Pro).

- **Icons:** Custom line icons for each of the five pillars. Simple, warm, not corporate.

- **Layout:** Generous whitespace. Large type. Short paragraphs. Scroll-driven storytelling. Animations on scroll (stats counting up, sections fading in).

 

## Mobile-First Principles

- Hero section must be powerful on a phone screen — many users will access via mobile on the South/West Side

- All CTAs must be thumb-reachable

- Intake form must work perfectly on mobile

- Knowledge Oasis content must load fast on mobile data connections

 

## Accessibility

- All text meets WCAG AA contrast ratios

- Alt text on all images

- Screen reader compatible

- Available in English and Spanish (minimum)

- Plain language throughout — target 6th-8th grade reading level for core content
