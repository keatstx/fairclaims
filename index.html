What We Discovered

Most of this money is already available to you.

  • Illinois law requires hospitals to provide free care if your family income is under 200% of the federal poverty level, and discounted care above that. Most qualifying families never apply. Most hospitals never tell them.
  • 80% of denied prior authorizations are overturned on appeal. But only 11.5% of patients ever appeal. Insurers are betting you'll give up.
  • Workers' compensation claims are denied 13–25% of the time on the first try and a significant percentage of "exclusive remedy" workplace compensation claims have access to up to 10X more compensation, often on technicalities or lack of disclosure.
  • Toxic exposure claims, from contaminated water, toxic products to PFAS "forever chemicals," represent billions in potential compensation. Families in industrial corridors on the South and West Side are disproportionately affected and dramatically underrepresented.
  • In February 2026, Illinois announced it had erased $1.1 billion in medical debt for 500,000+ residents using just $10 million in state funds. Every dollar spent cleared $100 in debt. That's what's possible when someone fights for you.

The system isn't broken. It's working exactly as designed.

We're here to redesign who it works for.

The Engine

A "Smart" Hub-and-Spoke Model, in 25 Neighborhoods

FairClaims won't operate from a downtown office. It will live where you live. Powered by the Chicago Urban League's ThriveAI Ecosystem, FairClaims deploys Community Equity Navigator (CEN) teams into each of the 25 lowest-income neighborhoods on Chicago's South and West Side.

Each CEN Team includes:

🧭
A Trusted Local Leader

Someone from your community who understands the culture, the challenges, and the people. A mature advocate who's already earned trust.

💻
A Youth Digital Native

A trained young leader recruited from Chicago's summer youth programs who can navigate the technology, the data, and the digital tools that power modern advocacy.

Together, they form your neighborhood's medical financial first responders.

The ecosystem behind them:

ThriveAI Wellness App

Your personal dashboard for tracking cases, understanding your rights, and accessing resources 24/7.

FairClaims Technology Platform (CKL)

The AI-augmented case management engine that powers advocacy at scale across all five pillars.

FairClaims Guild 2.0

A cooperative network of vetted attorneys, financial advocates, and healthcare navigators who've agreed to serve FairClaims families at fair, transparent rates.

Veripedia Knowledge Oasis

A rich, searchable library of guides, videos, and FAQs, built so every family can understand their rights, their options, and their next step.

The Next Generation

Creating the Next Lewis Latimers

In 1876, a young Black draftsman named Lewis Howard Latimer drew the patent illustrations that helped Alexander Graham Bell receive the patent for the telephone. He went on to work alongside Thomas Edison, translate technical data across languages, and invent a process that made electric lighting affordable for ordinary Americans.

Latimer wasn't born into opportunity. He created it through mastery.

The Latimer Leaders Program

FairClaims' youth development pathway transforms young people from Chicago's South and West Side into Medical Financial "Draftsmen", skilled digital advocates who can navigate complex systems, decode bureaucratic processes, and help their neighbors claim what's theirs.

"Latimer didn't just draw the blueprints for someone else's invention. He became indispensable. That's what we're building, a generation of young advocates who are too skilled, too knowledgeable, and too connected to be left behind."

Our Foundation

Love and Equity — Organizing for a New Era

The tradition of community organizing on Chicago's South and West Side runs deep, from the Back of the Yards to the present day.

FairClaims is built on that tradition. Not the old model of fighting against power from the outside, but a new model of building power from within.

We partner with organizers, faith leaders, block clubs, and existing community programs to embed medical financial advocacy into the infrastructure that already exists. We don't replace what's working. We amplify it.

What makes FairClaims different:

  • Federated, not centralized — Each neighborhood hub operates with local leadership, local knowledge, and local trust. The platform connects them. The community runs them.
  • Advocacy, not charity — We don't give families a handout. We give them a navigator, a network, and the knowledge to claim what's theirs.
  • Digital + Human — Technology handles the complexity. People handle the trust. Together, they bridge the digital divide and the debt divide at the same time.

When you organize around love, love for neighbors, for families, for the next generation, equity follows.
