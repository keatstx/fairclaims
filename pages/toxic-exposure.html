PILLAR 5 OF 5

If your neighborhood made you sick, someone owes you more than an apology.

From lead paint to contaminated water to PFAS "forever chemicals," communities on Chicago's South and West Side have been disproportionately exposed to industrial toxins for generations.

The Exposure Map

Environmental Injustice Is Medical Financial Injustice

  • Camp Lejeune: more than 410,000 administrative claims and nearly 3,000 lawsuits filed by veterans and family members exposed to contaminated drinking water.
  • AFFF firefighting foam containing PFAS "forever chemicals" has contaminated groundwater around fire stations, airports, and military bases nationwide, including sites across Cook County.
  • Chicago's South and West Side sit in the shadow of industrial corridors, scrap yards, petcoke piles, and decades of legacy lead contamination in soil, paint, and aging water service lines.
  • Mass tort and class action settlements move billions of dollars every year, yet South and West Side families are dramatically underrepresented because they are never told they qualify.
Products in the Environment

It is not just air and water. It is the products around you.

When people hear "toxic exposure," they think of factories and dump sites. The truth is broader. Many of the most damaging exposures come from everyday consumer and commercial products that ended up in homes, schools, workplaces, and the environment, where they continue to cause harm long after they were sold.

Product-driven exposures we screen for:

  • Lead paint, lead pipes, and lead solder in older homes, schools, and water service lines, linked to learning disabilities, neurological harm, and lifelong health costs.
  • PFAS "forever chemicals" in nonstick cookware, stain and water repellents, food packaging, cosmetics, and AFFF firefighting foam, now found in the blood of nearly every American.
  • Asbestos in insulation, brake pads, floor tiles, and joint compound, still causing mesothelioma and lung disease decades after exposure.
  • Talc-based powders linked to ovarian cancer and mesothelioma in long-term users.
  • Hair relaxers and chemical straighteners tied in recent studies to elevated risk of uterine and ovarian cancer, with disproportionate impact on Black women.
  • Glyphosate and agricultural pesticides connected to non-Hodgkin lymphoma and other cancers in workers, applicators, and nearby residents.
  • Diesel exhaust and petcoke dust from trucking corridors, rail yards, and storage piles concentrated near South and West Side neighborhoods.
  • Defective medical devices and pharmaceuticals, from hernia mesh to hormone therapies, that injure patients long after FDA clearance.

If you lived, worked, or grew up around any of these products, you may have a claim, even if you have never spoken to a lawyer. FairClaims screens your history against active mass tort dockets and connects qualifying families to vetted Guild attorneys.

Your Path to Recovery

How FairClaims Helps

  1. Exposure assessment. We map where you have lived, worked, and gone to school against known contamination sites and active product liability dockets.
  2. Claim eligibility screening. We match your medical history and exposure profile to the qualifying criteria for mass tort, class action, and environmental justice claims.
  3. Trusted legal connection. We refer eligible families to Guild attorneys at fair, transparent rates, never to predatory mass mailers.
  4. Ongoing monitoring. New settlements open every year. We keep your file active and alert you when a new claim window opens.
  5. Community-wide advocacy. We aggregate exposure data by block and ZIP code to support broader environmental justice action alongside community partners.
