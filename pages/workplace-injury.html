You got hurt at work. You deserve every dollar.

Workers' compensation exists to protect you, but the system is designed to pay as little as possible. We're here to make sure you get the maximum.

The Denial Playbook

A system designed to pay as little as possible.

  • 13 to 25% of workers' compensation claims are denied on first attempt, often for procedural reasons that can be cured on appeal.
  • Employers and supervisors quietly discourage reporting. Insurance carriers routinely attribute new injuries to pre-existing conditions to reduce payout.
  • Most injured workers do not know their rights, do not know they can appeal, and never speak to an attorney before signing.
  • Without an advocate, injured workers accept lowball settlements that fail to cover lifetime medical care, lost wages, or permanent impairment.
What Most Workers Are Never Told

The Exclusive Remedy Trap

In Illinois and nearly every other state, workers' compensation is the exclusive remedy for an on-the-job injury. In plain English, that means once your injury is covered by workers' comp, you generally cannot sue your employer for pain and suffering, emotional distress, or the full economic value of your loss. You receive a fixed schedule of benefits, and that is usually the end of it.

For many workers, that schedule is far less than what their injury is actually worth. A back injury that ends a career, a hand crushed in machinery, a fall that leads to chronic pain, all of it is funneled into a capped formula that rarely reflects real life.

Where the real money usually lives:

  • Third-party liability claims. If a contractor, equipment manufacturer, property owner, or another company outside your direct employer contributed to your injury, you may have a separate civil case in addition to workers' comp.
  • Defective product claims. Faulty machinery, defective safety gear, and unsafe tools open the door to product liability recovery against the manufacturer.
  • Toxic and repetitive exposure. Chemical burns, respiratory damage, and cumulative trauma injuries often involve multiple responsible parties beyond the employer.
  • Permanent partial and permanent total disability ratings. Many workers settle before a full impairment rating is established, leaving significant benefits on the table.
  • Vocational rehabilitation and future medical. Lifetime medical care and retraining benefits are routinely undervalued in quick settlements.

FairClaims helps injured workers see the full picture before they sign. We screen every case for third-party angles, product liability, and exposure claims that sit outside the exclusive remedy bar, so families recover everything the law actually allows.
