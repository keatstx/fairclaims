Pillar 1 of 5

You might qualify for free hospital care. Right now.

Under Illinois law, nonprofit hospitals must offer free care to families earning under 200% of the federal poverty level, and discounted care above that. Most families who qualify are never told.

Did You Know?

The law is on your side. Here's what hospitals aren't telling you.

  • For a family of four in 2025, 200% of the federal poverty level is approximately $62,400. If your household earns less than that, you may qualify for completely free hospital care.
  • Even above that income, Illinois law limits what hospitals can collect to 20% of your family income.
  • Hospitals are legally required to display financial assistance information in their admissions and emergency room areas, in your language. Most don't make it easy to find.
  • In Cook County alone, a program that spent $4 million in public funds erased nearly $500 million in medical debt, but investigations revealed most of that debt belonged to patients who should have qualified for free care in the first place.
Your Path to Relief

How FairClaims Helps

  1. We screen you — Our Navigator helps you determine if you qualify for charity care based on your income, family size, and the hospital where you received care.
  2. We complete the paperwork — Financial assistance applications can be confusing and intimidating. We walk you through every line.
  3. We advocate on your behalf — If a hospital is slow to respond, fails to offer required information, or denies a legitimate application, we escalate.
  4. We opt you in to ongoing protection — Once enrolled, your charity care status can protect you from future bills at the same institution.
