The Latimer Leaders Program — Become a Medical Financial Draftsman

Who Was Lewis Latimer?

Lewis Howard Latimer (1848–1928) was a Black inventor, draftsman, and engineer who drew patent illustrations for Alexander Graham Bell's telephone. He worked alongside Thomas Edison and invented the carbon filament process that made incandescent lighting practical and affordable. Remarkably, Latimer was self-taught while serving in the Union Navy—a testament to curiosity, determination, and the power of learning by doing.

The Modern Draftsman

Today's tools are AI platforms, data systems, and advocacy workflows. Latimer Leaders are young people training as medical financial advocates—learning to navigate complex systems and make them accessible to their communities.
